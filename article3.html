Empowering Women Through Sex Ed

Tiffani Tirta Chanjaya - 22 September 2021

Sex has always been traditionally regarded as a taboo subject to discuss. Many people seemed to ignore the fact how it could help young people, especially girls, to understand the changes that they will face with their bodies and mind.


The definition of sex ed itself is a curriculum-based approach of teaching and learning about the cognitive, emotional, physical, and social elements of sexuality is known as comprehensive sexuality education.

The goal is to provide children and young people with the knowledge, skills, attitudes, and values necessary to realize their health, well-being, and dignity; develop respectful social and sexual relationships; consider how their choices affect their own and others' well-being; and understand alongside with protecting their rights throughout their lives.

Sex is nothing to be ashamed of, it is part of a natural and healthy desire we have as a human being. Unfortunately, society frequently fosters a sense of shame in women, particularly young women, around sex, implying that women who are aware of and understand the act of intercourse are a women with negative influence.

It is also stated by Oxford Bibliographies that Education-based prevention initiatives, sometimes known as "sex education" programs, are one of the most effective ways to stop HIV/AIDS from spreading. Not only that, but according to Studies and Researchers from the University of Washington also finds that Comprehensive Sex Education Reduces Teen Pregnancy. Adolescents who receive the education are also reported to be much less likely to become pregnant than those who receive it only until marriage or no formal sex education at all.

In the Guardian, Chernor Bah, a global education activist and former Sierra Leone refugee, wrote, “As boys and men see more educated girls in their midst, I believe they will be more likely to abandon their archaic perceptions of women as inferior and mere sexual objects with no meaningful role to play outside the home.”

Thus, as stated. We arm women with the tools they need for life by providing them with an honest sexual education. We empower them to handle their own bodies, identities, and minds by not just teaching them how to protect themselves from AIDS, but also by teaching them about consent, sexual identity, their own anatomy, and pleasure.

AIn conclusion, we should abolish past believe that sex education is something unnececary. We women deserve the knowledge to protect ourselves, to understand the consequences and how to live a healthier lifestyle.
